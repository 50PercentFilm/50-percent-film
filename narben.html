Synopsis

"A moment suspended between memory and imagination."

Lina revisits a time marked by emptiness and quiet despair. As she encounters her younger self, a silent truth emerges: some wounds will heal, but their traces remain. Narben is a film about the meeting point between who we were and who we became — told through a structure that moves between the remembered and the imagined, never fully separating the two. At nine minutes, it holds more interior weight than films many times its length.

Director

Joshua Haiden

Director / Writer / Producer

Joshua Haiden is an Austrian filmmaker and the driving creative force behind Narben. Writing, directing and producing the film through his own production company, Haiden constructs a quietly devastating portrait of psychological recovery — one that trusts its audience to sit with ambiguity and find meaning in the space between memory and imagination.

On the Film

Memory.
Self.
Healing.

Narben operates in the space that most films avoid — the interior life of recovery, where progress is not linear and the self you left behind does not simply disappear. By staging a literal encounter between Lina and her younger self, Haiden finds a formal language for something usually left to words: the way we carry our past versions within us, not as weight alone, but as evidence of survival.
